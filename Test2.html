INTRODUCCIÓN


Nuevas reglamentaciones con respecto a la clasificación y medición de instrumentos financieros entrará en vigor desde de enero de 2018, aunque se permite la aplicación antes de esa fecha.

IFRS 9 Financial Instruments reemplazará

IAS 39 Financial Instruments: Recognition and Measurement



Regulación actual requiere pérdidas para ser reportado según los términos de IAS 39 – donde solamente pérdidas incurrida son reportadas en la Declaración de renta.



Los nuevas regulaciones según IFRS 9 requerirán un transición a un modelo de pérdida esperada, donde pérdidas de deterioro debido a cambios en expectativas de pérdida de crédito son reconocidos.

Entidades que mantienen activos a coste amortizado and all financial instruments subject to impairment accounting:


Incluyendo empresas comerciales e industriales que mantienen deudores comerciales;

Compañías de seguros que invierten dinero en activos financieros para cubrir sus pasivos por seguro.

INCURRED LOSS MODEL – IAS 39


  1. Initial NPV calculated solely from the expected contractual cash flows - without taking into account any possible credit losses.
  2. Expected losses are not shown on the Income Statement
  3. Only incurred impairment losses are used to calculate changes to the reported carry value of the asset.

  1. IAS 39 uses a slightly modified definition of what would traditionally be considered an impaired loss.

  2. For example, a credit rating downgrade would normally be reason to be considered an impairment (and reduction in report NPV of asset, due to the implied reduction in the present value of future cash flows). However this is explicitly stated not to be the case under IAS 39; "A downgrade of an entity’s credit rating is not, of itself, evidence of impairment”.

  1. Once an asset is considered impaired, the asset’s new carry value is calculated using the effective interest rate at initial recognition. Changes in credit worthiness or changes in likely hood of future default are not taken into account.

  2. For example, two missed payments would likely increase the probability of further default, however the effective interest rate is not adjusted accordingly.

RAZONES PARA LA TRANSICION AL MODELO NUEVO


  1. Durante la crisis financiera anterior, se aumentaron las críticas contra el modelo de pérdidas incurrida por activos financieros del IFRS.
  2. Pérdidas de deterioro y los cambios subsiguientes en el valor reportado, solo pueden ser reportados cuando hay evidencia que las pérdidas han sido incurridas efectivamente. Entidades de reporte no están autorizadas para considerar los efectos de pérdidas esperadas.

  1. Analistas e inversionistas se quejan de que los resultados financieros producidos por el modelo IAS 39 son generalmente confusos y no proveen suficiente información para comprender las actividades de gestión de riegos de empresas.
  2. El modelo nuevo proveerá mejor transparencia de la verdadera posición de estos activos financieros y permite a los accionistas y inversionistas ver como la empresa esta dirigiendo su riesgo.

  1. Example of how the ECB recognises debt (such as with Greece). Policy and decisions can be affected as Expected losses are not shown in the balance sheet, and hence there is an incentive to never make the debt vest/ become incurred. Accepting (incurring a loss) is worse politically and psychologicaly for any politician or executive. Eliminating physiological factors would be in the best interest of the EU or Shareholders.

PROPÓSITO Y ÁMBITO DEL MODELO NUEVO



  • Asegurar que un activo no es usado para informes financieros como un valor mayor que su verdadero valor.

  • Prevenir que la posición financiera y comportamiento de una entidad de reporte de sea exagerada.

EXPECTED LOSS MODEL – IFRS 9


  1. Initial NPV calculated from the expected future cash flows - Taking into account future credit losses.

  2. For example, the risk margin associated with an individual loan would be deducted from the instruments contractual effective interest rate.

  1. Impairment losses due to adverse changes in credit loss expectations are recognised.

  2. Impairment losses or gains are shown in a separate line item in the Income Statement when expectations change.
    There is no impairment trigger - evidence that losses have been incurred.

  1. Expected cash flows and expected losses are subject to periodical
    re-estimation.

  2. Does not incorporate unexpected losses outside a determined confidence interval.

COMPARISION OF THE TWO MODELS



  • Expected loss model is more subjective in nature compared to the incurred loss model, since it relies significantly on the cash flow estimates prepared by the reporting entity.
  • Expected loss model requires entities to utilise forward-looking data, in order to add further accuracy to default probabilities.

  • The expected loss model smooths losses over the life of the asset providing a consistent documented rate of return. Under the incurred loss model, the rate of return will appear artificially high at the beginning, diminishing through out its life.

MODELING EXPECTED CASH FLOWS



Model Inputs


IASB Exposure Draft requires that an entity shall estimate the expected cash flows considering:


  • All contractual terms of the financial instrument:
  • Prepayment
  • Call
  • and other similar options

  • Fees and points paid or received between parties to the contract that are an integral part of the effective interest rate (see IAS 18 Revenue) to the extent they are not included in the initial measurement of the financial instrument;

  • For financial assets, credit losses over the entire life of the asset.

Calculating Expected Losses


The calculation of expected losses on existing loans should incorporate management’s estimates, based on past and future loss events.

Suggested factors to consider include:


  • Internal/external historical credit loss experience

  • Internal or external credit ratings

  • External Reports and Statistics

  • Peer group experience for comparable financial assets

As well as...


  • Nature of the borrower

  • Product

  • Market

  • Economic Outlook

Manipulation of Inputs


  • Historical data such as credit loss experience should be adjusted on the basis of current observable data in order to reflect the effects of current conditions.

  • Estimation of the amounts and timings of cash flows need to take into account the concept of probability.

  • Expected losses based on probability-weighted possible outcomes should only include an estimate of the maximum loss that can be suffered based on what is expected to be lost on average in a time specific horizon and based on historical exposures.

  • The calculation does not incorporate losses outside a determined level of probability.

Actuarial Models used and example applications


Expected losses recognized once there has been a significant deterioration in credit quality/ credit risk of the lender.

3 Accounting stages:

Stage 1:
At origination entities are to report their 12-month expected credit losses.
- The probability of default occurring in the next 12 months multiplied by the total (lifetime) expected credit losses that would result from that default.

Stage 2:
When the credit risk increases significantly from that at origination and the resulting credit quality is considered below “investment grade”. Or once payments are more than 30 days over due, full lifetime expected credit losses are to be recognized.

Stage 3:
Once the credit quality of a financial asset deteriorates to the point where the asset is credit-impaired or losses are considered incurred. In Colombia, Panama, Costa Rica, Peru, Mexico a loss is considered incurred after 180 days.

REPORTING CONSISTENCY


A fair amount of judgement and discretion is involved in making expected credit loss estimates. Which could make it difficult for outsiders to fully interpret the meaning of such estimates.

Additionally Management’s estimates and calculation of expected losses will not necessarily be the same a regulator’s estimate.

Challenges


Onerous in data collection. Data needs to be collected for the whole portfolio. Transition Matrices also require copious amounts of data. Conditioning transition matrices to changing macro environments demands multiple economic cycles. Each cell in the matrix that has a material amount of loans needs to have sufficient observations to draw meaningful conclusions.

All entities may also have issues with extrapolating data. The recommendations of Basel II require only one year of expected loss data, while the expected loss model would require development of that data to maturity.
